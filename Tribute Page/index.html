J. Robert Oppenheimer

J. Robert Oppenheimer, photographed in 1944.
J. Robert Oppenheimer was an American theoretical physicist and the scientific director of the Manhattan Project, which produced the first nuclear weapons during World War II. His work played a crucial role in the development of the atomic bomb, forever changing the course of history. Oppenheimer later became an advocate for international control of nuclear weapons and a critic of the arms race. His legacy remains controversial due to his involvement in the atomic bomb's creation and the subsequent political fallout.
